Dr Who Fan Edits List

WHOFLIX is the original Doctor Who Fan Edits site.

This is where fan editing of the BBC TV series Doctor Who began back in 2007.

Twelve years later, all of the 281 TV stories from William Hartnell to Peter Capaldi have been fan edited by that mysterious Fan Editor in Time and Space known only as Whopix. His first fan edit, of The Five Doctors, was made on VHS in 1997, pre-dating The Phantom Edit.

If you include Non-Who edits, Non-TV Who Edits, The Sarah Jane Adventures, the Peter Cushing Dalek Movies, Audiobook & Soundtrack versions, the Orphan Episodes, Animations and all those stories that have been edited more than once, Whopix has actually completed 363 fan edits. That makes him the most experienced fan editor on the planet!

Donations towards helping cover the cost of hosting the files are welcome.

Chronologically, the final fan edit of the project is Twice Upon A Time.

        The idea behind Whoflix was to fan edit the Classic Series to bring it more in line with the New iteration. At its simplest:
        from any 25m CS episode, on average, there’s only about 10m where the story is moving forward.
        by contrast, with any NS episode, on average there’s only 10m where the story isn’t moving forward!

        In the early days of Whoflix NS edits were a real pain as I only had the vanilla releases, which were in stereo. I held off buying the box sets as, for the price, I didn’t feel the extras justified it. It was only later that I discovered how to extract each audio file from the 5:1 sound mix. That makes NS edits a heck of a lot easier!

        And with the Classic Series completed, and no new New Who until Xmas, I have plans to do some more if I can find the time once Uni has calmed down.

    Mr. Insignificant says:

    Have you any thoughts on making a fan edit of a missing serial with telesnaps?

    • +
      whopix says:
      April 26, 2018 at 8:42 pm

      I have thought about it, but I’m not convinced that the pain would be worth the gain. Doing the telecomics over at Whopix has given me an idea of just how much work would be involved, and it would be a lot – especially for something like The Myth Makers! I always find that the problem with telesnap recons is that you spend half your time trying to work out which other story they’ve screen grabbed the Doctor from, like Britbox mocking up the opening of The Wheel In Space using the start of The Mind Robber.

      And then there’s the fact that Loose Cannon have already done them all…

      I prefer doing the telecomic versions if I’m honest, but I’m not totally against the idea. Personally I like the idea of some sort of Motion Comic instead of static slides, but if enough people wanted to see Whoflix do fan edit recons then I’d certainly consider it. But only after I’ve finished the remaining edits for Tennant, Smiffy & Capaldi.
